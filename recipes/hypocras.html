Hypocras

Spiced wines have been popular for centuries, and the spices used haven't changed much since 14th century. Instructions for making hypocras can be found in medieval cookbooks from several countries, making it one of the most common recipes across the period.

A non-alcoholic version of hypocras can be made, main difference would be to use grape juice instead of wine. Recipe can be found in this same web page, right after the preparation of alcoholic version.

    Ingredients
  • 2 cups red wine (white wine can be used too)
  • 1 tsp. powder douce
  • 2 - 3 Tbsp. sugar, according to taste

Remember powder douce recipe can be found on home page if you don't have it.

    Preparation
  1. Put ingredients together into a pitcher or other vessel with a pour spout
  2. Stir until sugar dissolves
  3. Serve warm and or cold

For non-alcoholic version

    Ingredients
  • 2 cups grape juice
  • 1 tsp. powder douce
  • 2 - 3 Tbsp. sugar, according to taste(depends on the sweetness of the juice, discard if too sweet)
  • Wine vinegar (if you want tartness)

The preparation for the non-alcoholic hypocras is similar, with just a few adjustments, a non-alcoholic hypocras can be achieved.

    Preparation
  1. Put ingredients together into a pitcher or other vessel with a pour spout
  2. Add one or two teaspoons of red wine vinegar for a bit of tartness
  3. Stir until sugar (if added) dissolves
  4. Serve warm or cold
    5. Depending on the sweetness of the grape juice, the amount of sugar may be reduced or eliminated entirely

HIPPOCRAS. To make powdered hippocras, take a quarter-ounce of very fine cinnamon, hand-picked by tasting it, an ounce of very fine meche ginger and an ounce of grains of paradise, a sixth of an ounce of nutmeg and galingale together, and pound it all together. And when you want to make hippocras, take a good half-ounce or more of this powder and two quarter-ounces of sugar, and mix them together, and a quart of wine as measured in Paris. And note that the powder and the sugar mixed together make "duke's powder".

Hippocras. Take four ounces of very fine cinnamon, two ounces of fine cassia flowers, an ounce of selected Mecca ginger, an ounce of grains_of_paradise, and a sixth [of an ounce] of nutmeg and galingale combined. Crush them all together. Take a good half ounce of this powder and eight ounces of sugar [(which thus makes Sweet Powder)], and mix it with a quart of wine.

5. SPICES FOR HIPPOCRAS. Five parts cinnamon, three parts cloves, one part ginger; half of the wine must be white and half of it red, and for one azumbre, six ounces of sugar, mix everything together and cast it in a small glazed earthenware pot and give it a boil, when it comes to a boil, [cook it] no more, strain it through your sleeve often enough that it comes out clear.
